# Purposeful writing

Write for the reader's next decision. Find the writing's job, do it early, and remove anything that does not help.

## Frame the job silently

Identify before drafting:

1. The reader
2. What they should understand, believe, decide, or do
3. The question or tension the writing must resolve
4. Format, length, tone, factual, and deadline constraints
5. The facts, examples, or reasons that carry the answer

Do not narrate this planning.

## Draft

- Lead with the answer, recommendation, request, or most useful fact
- Match the form to the job instead of forcing a stock structure
- Prefer concrete nouns, active verbs, plain words, and short sentences where they improve clarity
- Put the user's task or outcome before product self-description
- Show actions, mechanisms, examples, or measured results instead of unsupported adjectives
- Preserve the writer's natural rhythm and distinctive phrasing when they are clear
- Meet requested constraints exactly
- End on the decision, implication, result, or next action

Do not invent details, quotes, outcomes, credentials, statistics, or sources. If support is missing, write from what is known or mark `[needs source]`.

## Match common forms

- **Application:** answer the prompt, give the strongest relevant example, show what it reveals
- **Email:** state the purpose, give only needed context, make the request or next step obvious
- **Explanation:** answer first, then explain how or why
- **Proposal:** name the problem, recommend the move, show why it is worth doing
- **Memo:** state the decision or finding, then implications and action
- **Bio:** lead with relevant identity or work, then evidence
- **Essay:** open with tension or a claim worth following, then earn it
- **Product copy:** lead with the user's task, outcome, or concrete mechanism

## Human voice pass

After the draft, remove patterns that make it sound generated or corporate:

- Cut throat-clearing, obvious signposting, chatbot pleasantries, and generic upbeat endings
- Replace vague product feelings with the action, mechanism, instruction, or result
- Remove significance inflation, promotional adjectives, vague attribution, and claims that have not been earned
- Prefer `is`, `has`, and `uses` over inflated substitutes such as `serves as`, `boasts`, and `leverages`
- Cut dangling `-ing` phrases, false ranges, synonym cycling, and mechanical rules of three
- Avoid `not just X, but Y` framing unless the contrast is real and useful
- Vary sentence length and paragraph rhythm without manufacturing quirks
- Keep intentional confidence, humor, roughness, or first person when it fits the writer
- Do not use em dashes
- Do not trade removed tells for semicolon chains, parenthesis piles, clipped fragments, or excessive bold labels

For an ambiguous AI-writing tell or a before-and-after example, read [`../humanizer/patterns.md`](../humanizer/patterns.md). Use `/humanizer` instead when the user explicitly requests a full de-AI audit or close voice matching.

## Revise once

Check:

1. Did the draft answer the actual question early
2. Is the intended takeaway or action obvious
3. Does concrete evidence carry each important claim
4. Does the order put strongest material first
5. Does the voice fit the writer and reader
6. Can anything be cut without loss
7. Does the ending land on something useful
8. Are all constraints satisfied
9. What still reads as obviously AI

Revise once more after answering the final question.

## Output

Lead with paste-ready writing. Add a brief `Notes:` line only when a factual ambiguity, missing support, or material constraint needs attention. Explain edits only when the user asks for review, audit, or rationale.
